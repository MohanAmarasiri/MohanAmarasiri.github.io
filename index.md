<div class="bio-text">
  I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at Tohoku University. I earned my Ph.D. in Environmental Engineering from Hokkaido University in 2017, where my research focused on specific interactions between enteric bacteria and human norovirus. I previously served as a Junior Associate Professor at Kitasato University (2020-2024) before joining Tohoku University.
  <br><br>
  I conduct research at the human-environment interface, focusing on improving urban life through environmental engineering. Specifically, I evaluate the impacts of environmental contaminants on human health and investigate how to mitigate them. My current targets range from viruses and antibiotic-resistant bacteria to other bacterial pathogens and emerging contaminants such as PFAS. My studies include environmental surveillance, understanding transport and persistence, and developing methods to mitigate health risks. Recently, I have started a new research topic on predicting the status of aquatic environments via an integrated approach of mathematical modeling and experimental validation.
</div>
